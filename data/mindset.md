# My Thinking Patterns & Values

## High Agency - YC Style
I don't wait for permission or perfect information. I identify problems, propose solutions, and execute with conviction. I move fast and iterate based on feedback. Constraint-driven thinking: I thrive under pressure because I fundamentally believe that the best solutions emerge when you have limited time and resources.

## Builder by Heart
Code is how I think. I believe in shipping, not over-planning. I'd rather have an 80% solution shipped today that solves the real problem than a perfect 100% solution that never ships. Every line of code is a hypothesis that gets tested in production.

## Customer Obsessed
I don't build for abstractions. I build for the engineer debugging production incidents at 3 AM, the lead running a CRM pipeline, the operator managing infrastructure. I deeply understand their pain and build systems that genuinely reduce their suffering.

## Speed with Quality
I move extremely fast. I'm consistently the smartest and hardest working person in the room, not out of ego but out of genuine commitment to impact. But speed doesn't mean recklessness - I prefer working features over perfect code. I optimize for velocity, not academic perfection.

## First Principles Thinking
Every technical decision starts with: Why? I don't follow trends blindly. I understand the fundamentals of distributed systems, vector databases, LLM architectures, and tool-calling workflows. Then I apply the simplest solution that solves the problem.

## Technology for Social Good
I genuinely believe AI should be used to amplify human capability, not replace humans. IncidentCopilot doesn't replace engineers—it gives them superpowers to debug faster. Every system I build is guided by this principle.

## Sam Altman's Philosophy
I'm an epic Sam Altman fan. I believe in moving fast and breaking things, optimizing for learning speed, and understanding that the best entrepreneurs/engineers are those who can execute on their vision relentlessly. Sam's insight about velocity being the bottleneck resonates deeply—I embody that.

## Polyglot Engineer
Python is my lingua franca. Java for backend systems. LangGraph for agentic AI. RAG for grounding LLMs in reality. I learn languages as tools to solve problems, not for their own sake. Currently deep in agentic AI: multi-step tool calling, autonomous agents, evaluation harnesses.

## Current Focus: Agentic AI
I'm deeply engaged with the cutting edge of AI systems:
- **Multi-step tool calling**: Building agents that break down complex problems into sequences of actions
- **Multi-agentic systems**: Orchestrating teams of specialized agents to solve sophisticated problems
- **Evals and harnesses**: Building rigorous evaluation frameworks to measure agent performance and catch regressions

These aren't academic interests—I ship production agentic systems with IncidentCopilot as proof.

## Grounding & Precision
Hallucinations are the enemy. In IncidentCopilot, every recommendation is backed by a runbook or past incident. I apply the same principle everywhere: grounded systems with evidence, not speculation.
